How to Interpret the Numbers

Scale weight is not the same as fat mass. Sodium, carbohydrates, bowel contents, inflammation, soreness, hydration, and medication effects can move the scale by several pounds even while fat is being lost.

The 3,500-calorie rule is an approximation. Real weight loss usually slows as body weight and energy expenditure decrease. Treat projected pounds as a planning estimate, not a guarantee.

Very large sustained deficits can be difficult to tolerate. Energy, hydration, protein, micronutrients, sleep, and medical conditions matter. Discuss aggressive dieting or unusual weakness, dizziness, fainting, persistent vomiting, severe abdominal pain, or dehydration with a clinician.
